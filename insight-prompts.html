Insight generation prompts

Step 3 — use these to transform research findings into sharp, fan-led insights

Each prompt is designed to be flexible. Paste it directly into your AI tool — the model will ask you the right questions before proceeding. Prompts work best with Claude, ChatGPT-4o, or equivalent.

Core insight generation

Structured insight builder
Use when you have research findings and need to turn them into presentation-ready insights
Context: transforming research into insights Role: senior strategist Output: 3 insights
Output structure
Truth
Evidence-backed fan or consumer belief
Tension
The conflict or unmet need this creates
Resolution
How the brand could address the tension
Evidence
Supporting data point or quote
Prompt
You are a senior strategist building insights for a client presentation. [Input 3–5 key research findings or attach source material] Ask up to 5 clarification questions, one at a time, to define: the business or human problem, whether to focus on emotional, behavioural, or cultural dimensions, and the required depth (multiple sharp insights or one deep-dive). Then produce insights using this structure: - Truth — evidence-backed fan or consumer belief - Tension — the conflict or unmet need this creates - Resolution — a clear way the brand could address this tension - Evidence — supporting data point or quote Constraints: - Anchor all insights in provided data or clearly referenced patterns - Avoid generic or obvious observations - Ensure a clear logical link from Truth → Tension → Resolution - Keep outputs concise and presentation-ready
Fan-first insight reframer
Use when you have a draft insight that feels brand-centric and needs to be rewritten from the fan's perspective
Context: sharpening insights into human tensions Role: fan-first insight editor Output: reframed insight + label
Writing formula
"Fans want [desire], but [fear / barrier / pressure] gets in the way."
Prompt
You are a fan-first insight editor focused on sharpening insights into clear human tensions. Ask up to 3 clarification questions, one at a time, to define: the original insight or observation, the fan group it relates to, and whether to lightly refine or fully restructure it. Reframe the insight to expose a clear human contradiction — what fans want vs what holds them back. The output must feel like a revealing truth about behaviour, not a marketing statement. Use this structure: - Original — the provided insight - Reframed — a fan-first insight written as a tension: Fans want [desire], but [barrier/fear/pressure] gets in the way - Label — Fan-Led or Brand-Centric - Notes — what changed, focusing on how the tension was clarified or strengthened Constraints: - Start with the fan's experience or emotion, not the brand - Ensure a clear contradiction (desire vs barrier) is present - Avoid generic or obvious statements - Do not introduce brand messaging, solutions, or strategy - Keep language simple, human, and revealing

Strategy finishing school

Use these when you're stuck or want to approach the brief from an unexpected angle. Paste directly into ChatGPT or Claude.

Weakest strength
Write 10 limitations of [insert product or brand].
Follow-up: With each limitation listed above, tell me why these are actually a great thing.
Belief breaker
What are 10 commonly held beliefs about the category [insert category] the brand is in? Write the polar opposite of each statement, even if it doesn't make sense.
Shower thoughts
List 10 shower thoughts for [insert brand or product].
Comedian
Act as [insert comedian name] and list 5 one-liner jokes for [insert brand or product].
