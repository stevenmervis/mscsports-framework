Mscsports Insights Framework

Brief-driven strategy process — click any step to expand

01

Define the problem

Clarify what you're actually solving for before any research begins

▼

Key questions

  • What do we need to know and what are we trying to solve?
  • What is the business problem — as the client sees it?
  • What is the human problem — what's stopping fans from engaging or buying?
  • What type of strategy is required? (Partnership / Campaign / Messaging / Content / Comms / Sales)

Defining the human problem

  • What are audiences not doing?
  • What belief-based, identity-based, emotional, or contextual frictions exist?
→ Output: a clearly defined business problem, human problem, and strategy type
02

Research

Gather audience, brand, and category evidence at the depth the brief requires

▼

Always do

Desktop research GWI basic data AI basic prompts

Additional options

Fan interviews / IDIs Social listening Focus groups Surveys / polls On-site observation Telmar Google Trends Past campaign data AI deep prompts

Research focus areas — expand each for questions

Audience ▼
  • Who are the core and potential fans — demographically, psychographically, and behaviourally?
  • What drives their passion for this sport or team, and what role does it play in their identity or lifestyle?
  • How do they currently engage with the sport — live, broadcast, digital, social, community?
  • What barriers or tensions prevent deeper engagement or connection with the sport or brand?
  • What unmet needs, motivations, or aspirations could the brand uniquely tap into through sponsorship?
Brand ▼
  • How is the brand currently perceived within the context of sport and among fans?
  • What are the brand's key business and marketing objectives that sponsorship should help achieve?
  • What aspects of the brand's purpose, values, or personality align with the spirit of sport or the target audience?
Category ▼
  • Which brands dominate the space, and what territories or narratives have they claimed?
  • What challenger brands or new entrants are redefining how sponsorship shows up?
  • What category conventions or clichés could we break to stand out meaningfully?
  • Where are the white spaces or untapped opportunities that no brand is currently owning?
Culture ▼
  • What cultural shifts or conversations are influencing the world of sport right now?
  • How can the brand credibly participate in or lead one of these cultural conversations?
  • What social or community issues connected to the sport could the brand authentically support?
  • What existing narratives or stereotypes in sport could the brand challenge or modernise?
  • How does this sport intersect with broader cultural spaces — music, fashion, entertainment, digital fandom — that the brand can leverage?

Credibility check for sources

  • Who conducted it? Is the source reputable?
  • How was the data collected?
  • How large was the sample?

03

Synthesise findings into insights

Move from what you found to why it matters

▼

Process

  • Summarise your research findings clearly
  • Identify patterns, tensions, or contradictions in the data
  • Reframe as a human truth — what people feel, believe, or need
  • Write the insight: simple, emotive, directional

Insight structure

Truth
Evidence-backed fan or consumer belief
Tension
The conflict or unmet need this creates
Resolution
How the brand could address the tension
Evidence
Supporting data point or quote

Writing formula

"Fans want [desire], but [fear / barrier / pressure] gets in the way."

Writing process

  • Draft — get the thought down plainly first
  • Rewrite — use visual words, add emotional tension with "but"
  • Test — share with others; you want an "aha" reaction

04

Stress test

Make sure what you have is actually an insight — not just an observation

▼

Three golden rules

New?Does this reveal a "why" that hasn't been explored before?
Actionable?Does it tell us why something is happening so we can do something about it?
Fan-led?Is it about fans, or does the solution it informs benefit fans?

Translate to action
