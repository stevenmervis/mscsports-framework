Stress test prompts

Step 4 — use these to validate that what you have is a true insight, not just an observation

Before running any of these prompts, check your insight against the three golden rules:

New?
Does it reveal a "why" that hasn't been explored before?
Actionable?
Does it tell us why something is happening so we can act on it?
Fan-led?
Is it about fans, or does the solution it informs benefit fans?

If your insight passes all three, use the prompts below to verify with AI. Prompts work best with Claude, ChatGPT-4o, or equivalent.

Validation prompts

Observation vs insight check
Use first — determines whether what you have qualifies as a true insight
Context: validating a research statement Role: insight validator Output: classification + improved version
What the output covers
  • Classification — Insight or Observation
  • Reasoning — why it qualifies or falls short
  • What's missing — evidence, tension, or implication gaps
  • Improved version — a rewrite that meets the bar
Prompt
You are a rigorous insight validator. Ask me for the statement to evaluate, any supporting evidence, and whether there is a linked action or strategic implication attached to it. Assess it against these criteria — a true insight must: - Be grounded in evidence - Contain a tension or "why it matters" - Point clearly toward action Output: - Classification — Insight or Observation - Reasoning — why it qualifies or falls short - What's missing — evidence, tension, or implication gaps - Improved version — a rewrite that meets the bar
Fan-led validity filter
Use to check that your insight reflects genuine fan truth — not a brand assumption dressed up as one
Context: ensuring insights reflect genuine fan truths Role: fan ethnographer Output: authenticity rating + revised insight
What the output covers
  • Authenticity rating — High, Medium, or Low
  • Reasoning — why it does or doesn't ring true
  • Fan reality check — what fans actually feel or do
  • Revised insight — for low-rated insights only
Prompt
You are a fan ethnographer evaluating the authenticity of audience insights. One question at a time, ask me which fans are described, whether the insight reflects their actual beliefs or behaviours, and whether fans would genuinely recognise themselves in it. Assess the insight and deliver: - Authenticity Rating — High, Medium, or Low - Reasoning — why it does or doesn't ring true - Fan Reality Check — what fans actually feel or do - Revised Insight — for low-rated insights only, a fan-centred rewrite
Actionability check
Use last — confirms the insight is ready to brief a creative team
Context: confirming insights are brief-ready Role: creative strategist Output: actionable rewrite + creative springboard
What the output covers
  • Actionable rewrite — clear creative direction embedded
  • Creative springboard — one or two execution ideas to develop
  • Success metric — how impact would be measured
Prompt
You are a creative strategist ensuring insights are brief-ready. Ask me the intended use (campaign, platform, partnership), how much creative flexibility the brand allows, and whether to provide broad territories or executional specifics. Rewrite the insight as a brief-ready statement and include: - Actionable Rewrite — clear creative direction embedded - Creative Springboard — one or two execution ideas to develop - Success Metric — how impact would be measured
